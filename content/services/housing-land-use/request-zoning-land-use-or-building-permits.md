# Building Permits, Zoning, and Land Use — Bacolod City

Apply for building permits, zoning clearances, and land use permits through the **Office of the Building Official (OBO)** and **City Engineer's Office (CEO)**.

---

## 1. Services Available

| Service                  | Office                          | Purpose                                            |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Building permit          | Office of the Building Official | Required for new construction, renovation, repair  |
| Zoning clearance         | City Planning & Development     | Verifies land use compliance with zoning ordinance |
| Occupancy permit         | Office of the Building Official | Certifies building is safe for occupancy           |
| Demolition permit        | Office of the Building Official | Required for demolishing structures                |
| Electrical permit        | Office of the Building Official | For electrical installations and repairs           |
| Plumbing/sanitary permit | Office of the Building Official | For plumbing and sanitary installations            |

---

## 2. Requirements for Building Permit

**For New Construction:**

- Accomplished application form
- Certified true copy of land title or tax declaration
- Zoning clearance from CPDO
- Building plans and specifications (signed by licensed architect/engineer)
- Structural plans (for multi-story buildings)
- Electrical and plumbing plans
- Fire safety evaluation clearance (from BFP)
- Sanitary permit (from City Health Office)
- Barangay clearance
- Valid IDs of owner and professionals

**For Renovation/Repair:**

- Application form
- Proof of ownership (title, tax declaration)
- Plans and specifications (if major renovation)
- Barangay clearance
- Valid IDs

---

## 3. Fees

Building permit fees are based on:

- Total floor area
- Type of construction (residential, commercial, industrial)
- Estimated project cost

**Typical Fees:**

- Small residential (up to 50 sqm): ₱2,000-₱5,000
- Medium residential (50-100 sqm): ₱5,000-₱15,000
- Large residential (100+ sqm): ₱15,000-₱50,000+
- Commercial buildings: Based on project cost

**Note:** Fees include building permit, electrical permit, plumbing permit, and other related fees.

---

## 4. How to Apply

### Step 1: Get Zoning Clearance

- Visit **City Planning & Development Office (CPDO)**
- Submit land title and location plan
- Pay zoning clearance fee
- Receive zoning clearance (1-3 days)

### Step 2: Prepare Building Plans

- Hire licensed architect or engineer
- Prepare building plans, structural, electrical, plumbing plans
- Plans must comply with National Building Code and local ordinances

### Step 3: Get Fire Safety Clearance

- Submit plans to **Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)**
- BFP evaluates fire safety compliance
- Receive fire safety evaluation clearance

### Step 4: Submit to OBO

- Go to **Office of the Building Official**
- Submit all requirements
- OBO reviews plans for compliance
- Pay building permit fees
- Receive building permit

**Processing Time:** 7-20 working days (depending on project complexity)

## 6. Important Reminders

- **Secure building permit before construction** - building without permit is illegal
- **Display permit** - building permit must be displayed at construction site
- **Inspections** - OBO will conduct inspections during construction
- **Occupancy permit** - required before occupying new building
- **Validity** - building permit valid for 1 year from issuance
- **Hire licensed professionals** - architects and engineers must be PRC-licensed

---

## 7. Penalties for Violations

Building without permit or violating building code:

- Stop work order
- Fines and penalties
- Demolition order (for illegal structures)
- Criminal charges (for serious violations)
